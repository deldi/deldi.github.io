I'd Give You A Halo

This song is one that is incredibly personal to me. I wrote it on a chilly autumn night after I heard that a friend had placed themselves into a dire situation, and I was unsure if they were alright.

I told myself that, if my friend was still alive after all of this, I would show this song to them. To let them know that, should they ever again think so lowly of themselves that they can’t dig their way out of whatever pit they’re in, I will always be willing to pour love and affirmation upon them to convince them otherwise. To let them know that, with everything that I know about their imperfect, flawed self, there’s still so much beauty in their person.

In this way, I hope that this song sets the stage for what I want my music to be for people. I have myself recently been going through a period of emotional turmoil due to a variety of circumstances, and through that season, I’ve experienced a myriad of intense feelings and mentality-shifting discoveries that I’ve taken to documenting in a series of songs as a form of soul care. While these songs expose this incredibly bare, vulnerable side of me, I hope that by putting them out there for people to stumble upon, somebody is able to connect with, learn from, and relate to my experiences so that maybe they won't feel so alone.

So, to those out there reading this, if you're ever in a position where you think so lowly of yourself that you want to give up, know that we are our own harshest critics, and as such, we tend to overlook the beauty present within us. So much so that, sometimes, we convince ourselves that it's not there at all.

But there is always beauty within you. And when you forget that, please be reminded that, in the eyes of those who care about you, there is much to give you a halo for. Even when you don't feel like it.
